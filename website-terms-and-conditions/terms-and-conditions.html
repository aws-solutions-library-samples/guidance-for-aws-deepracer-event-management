4.   HOW TO ENTER:

a.    You must register at the track where you will need to provide the following details: title, name, surname, alias, country, email, and phone number) to an AWS employee and accept these Terms & Conditions.

b.    You can either bring a USB stick with your own trained Reinforcement Learning model, or you can select an AWS DeepRacer Reinforcement Learning model from the ‘model selector’ at the AWS Summit.

c.     Your Reinforcement Learning model then will be loaded onto a randomly selected AWS DeepRacer car and you will have three minutes to record your fastest valid lap time (as recorded by the AWS employee). Your lap time will then be entered into the live race leader board along with your rank and alias.

d.    After the race the race results will be displayed (alias, rank, lap time, country) on the AWS DeepRacer League website.

e.     Summit Circuit events can be very busy. We cannot guarantee that you will be able to race and if you do, depending on numbers, you may be restricted to only one race. However, if there is capacity you may race more than once and only your best time will be kept.

6.   AWS DeepRacer League Summit Circuit race rules. The rules below may vary per race, and if they do they will be communicated at track side, but generally the following will apply. To obtain a valid lap time in a Summit Circuit race, you must follow these rules:

a.    You may only race with a stock AWS DeepRacer from the devices made available by AWS at the race. The device provided is at the sole discretion of the AWS DeepRacer Pit Boss, and substitutions will not be made. Each device has been tested and calibrated by our pit bosses to work within consistent mechanical and network conditions, no unit has been identified as advantageous or suboptimal.

b.    All timings shall be as determined by an AWS employee using a stop-watch, timekeeping app, or other timing device, and all times are final.

c.    Each race attempt will last for 3 minutes (unless otherwise stated at track side). During your attempt, you may restart your lap as often as you like, while the 3-minute (or other duration) race attempt timer is running. If your timer runs out in the middle of a lap that lap will not be counted. Once your timer has run-out your attempt is over. You can queue again for another race attempt, subject to availability and the track being open.

d.    For a lap to be valid, the car has to complete one lap around the entire track following the indicated direction of the track, starting when the front wheels of the car touch the starting line (when moving), or standing on the line (when starting from standstill), and touching the starting line again with the front wheels of the car. If the race timer has run out and no valid lap has been completed, you will not have a valid lap time to submit and be marked as DNF. Your DNF attempt will not be listed on the leader board.

e.    If all four wheels of the car is on the field (green surface and considered off the track), an AWS employee will use their reasonable best efforts while acting in a safe and controlled manner to reset the device at the last good point on the track, that is, the last point where it touched the track or borders. During a reset, the lap timer will not be stopped, it will keep running.

f.      If your car starts driving in the wrong direction during the race, an AWS employee will use their reasonable best efforts while acting in a safe and controlled manner to reset the device at the last good point on the track. Racers are required to stop vehicle if driving in the wrong direction.

g.    Once the first lap of an attempt has been initiated, the race attempt timer will not be paused or restarted due to operator or model malfunction. Attempts interrupted due to mechanical malfunction that are not the direct result of the operator or model causing mechanical damage to the device during an attempt shall be awarded at the sole discretion of the official AWS DeepRacer Track Boss.

h.     If the vehicle encounters an issue during an attempt, our track and pit bosses will use reasonable best efforts to troubleshoot and return the car to the starting line to continue, until the countdown timer has expired. If the pit crew is unable to remedy the issue before time runs out, that attempt will be considered completed and no additional time shall be granted.
